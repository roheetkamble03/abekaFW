  • Essay - Question 12
    Explain how rain forms by the collision-coalescence process.

    To complete this type of question, type the answer below in the space provided. Answers should be given using complete sentences and placed in paragraph form. These types of questions will be graded later by a teacher and points will be awarded for correctly and completely answering the question as well as for correct grammar usage. Items may also be graded on focus, detail, and organization. Just above the answer box is a tool bar with a few tools that you may use including undo last 30s and redo last 30s buttons.

    Rain forms by the collision-coalescence process in clouds with temperatures above freezing such as in the Tropics. Larger than normal condensation nuclei cause the formation of giant cloud droplets. As these droplets drift downward, they collide with smaller droplets and stick to them, growing bigger in the process. Eventually, the growing droplets become too big to remain suspended in the air and they begin to fall.

  • Measuring - Question 25
    Use the protractor and ruler tools to measure the number of degrees in this angle.
         

         
     
        
    Let′s use two more of the tools under the Tools icon above to answer this question. Click the Tools icon  and choose the protractor . The double-sided red curved arrow on the left corner can be used to rotate the protractor to match the angle you are measuring. The smaller double-sided arrow at the right corner can be used to make the protractor bigger or smaller. Try out those two features and then use the protractor to measure the degrees in the angle above by putting the center point at the point of the angle.

    A second tool under the Tools icon is the ruler. Click on the 15 cm Ruler . The double-sided red curved arrow on the left side can be used to rotate the ruler as well. You will notice if you rotate the ruler all the way upside down that there are inch measurements on the other side. This tool may be helpful for measuring the angle above by rotating it to use the straight edge. You can then carry out the upper line segment of the angle until it meets the angle marking lines on the protractor. The red x for each tool will close them.

    The answer is 47°. The degree symbol is in the character palette.
